[[manufacturer:gottlieb]]'s first solid-state pinball system, introduced in 1977, based on the Rockwell PPS-4, a 4-bit microprocessor. It debuted with [[title:cleopatra]] (1977) and was used on titles like [[title:close-encounters]] (1978) and [[title:sinbad]] (1978). The unusual choice of a 4-bit CPU made these boards distinctive among competitors who favored 8-bit Motorola processors.
