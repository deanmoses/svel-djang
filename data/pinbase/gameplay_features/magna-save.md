A patented [[manufacturer:williams]] mechanism that uses an electromagnet beneath the playfield to grab and hold a steel ball as it rolls toward the outlane. The player activates the magnet by pressing a dedicated button — separate from the [[gameplayfeature:flippers]] buttons — at precisely the right moment. If the timing is correct, the ball sticks to the playfield surface long enough for gravity to pull it back toward the flippers. Magna-Save was introduced on *[[title:black-knight]]* in 1980 and appeared on a handful of subsequent [[manufacturer:williams]] titles. It remains one of pinball's most dramatic save mechanics: a physical intervention that feels like cheating the laws of physics.
