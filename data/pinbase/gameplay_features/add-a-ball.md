An alternative reward system in which the player earns an additional ball of play rather than a free game. Add-a-ball emerged as a response to local regulations that classified [[gameplayfeature:replay]] awards as gambling: if the machine never awarded a free game, it could not be considered a gambling device. The distinction was legally significant — it determined whether a machine could be placed on location in a given jurisdiction. Machines configured for add-a-ball play typically displayed different artwork and messaging to make the distinction clear to both players and regulators.
